How Innerwine came to be

Two siblings.
One idea.

Innerwine began with a conversation between a brother and sister — the kind that starts with a glass of wine and ends somewhere unexpected. One of them had spent eleven years as a certified sommelier, moving through restaurant floors, private cellars, and vineyards across Europe. The other had spent nineteen years in strategy, sales, and marketing — building brands, understanding people, and asking why things work the way they do.

The question they kept returning to was the same one: why does wine recommendation always start with food? Why does nobody ask how you're feeling? The sommelier had always known that the right bottle for a Tuesday evening of quiet exhaustion is nothing like the right bottle for a celebration. The strategist saw that nobody had ever built that insight into a product.

That gap — between eleven years of emotional intuition about wine and nineteen years of knowing how to turn an idea into something real — is where Innerwine was born.

Beyond the app

A novel is coming.
About the things we feel.

In a small square in Taormina, four friends meet twice a year. Same table. Same ritual. One bottle of wine. One rule: speak from the gut. What follows are four confessions — raw, intimate, and impossible to take back.

Stories of love that arrived too late. Truths buried for years. Grief carried in silence. The courage to finally be seen. Each story has a wine. Each wine remembers the moment the truth came out.
